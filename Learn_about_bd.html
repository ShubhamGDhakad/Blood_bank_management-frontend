Types of Blood Donation

The average human body contain about five liters of Blood,which is made of several cellular and non-cellular components such as Red blood cells,platelet and Plasma
Each type of component has its unique properties and can be used for different indications. The donated blood is seperated into these components by the blood centre and one donated unit can save upto foour lives depending on the number of components seperated from your blood



what is it?
Blood collected straight from the donor into a blood bag and mixed

with an anticoagnulant is called as whole blood.This collected

whole blood is then centrifuged and red cells, platelates and

plasma are seperated. The seperated Red cells are mixed with a

preservative to be called as packed red blood cells.
who can donate?
You need to be 18-65 years old,weight 45kg or more and be

fit and healthy.
User For?
correction of severe anemia in a number of conditions and blood

loss in case of child birth, sergery or trauma settings.
Lasts For?
Red cells can be stored for 42 days at 2-6 degree celsius.
How long does it takes to donate?
15-30 minutes to donate. including the pre-donation check-up.
How often can i donate?
male donars can donate again after 90 days and female donars

can donate again after 120 days
